About

What is INNOGENESIS?

INNOGENESIS 2026 is NRI University's flagship intercollegiate hackathon — a 24-hour marathon of ideation, prototyping, and execution. Held at Dr. RVR NRI Institute of Technology campus in Agiripalli, the event brings together the brightest student innovators from across India.

The name says it all: INNO (innovation) + GENESIS (the origin, the beginning). This is where your boldest idea gets its first breath of life — guided by industry mentors, fueled by caffeine, backed by cash prizes worth up to ₹5 Lakhs, and accompanied by exciting entertainment.

Whether you're a solo coder, a design duo, or a full-stack team of four — INNOGENESIS is your stage. No experience level required. Just bring the drive to build something real.

FAQ

Got questions?
We've got answers.

Who can participate in INNOGENESIS?
Any currently enrolled undergraduate or postgraduate student from any college in India. No restriction on year, branch, or specialization. You just need a valid college ID.
What is the team size?
Teams can have 2 to 4 members. If you're applying solo, mark yourself as "looking for a team" and we'll connect you with other solo participants before the event. Purely solo participation is not permitted during the hackathon itself.
Is there a registration fee?
The registration fee is ₹500 per participant. This covers the 24-hour hackathon — meals, snacks, beverages, WiFi, and event swag are all included throughout the event.
Can teams have members from different colleges?
Yes! Cross-college teams are allowed and encouraged. Each team member must individually submit valid college identification during check-in on August 7.
Do we need to submit a project idea beforehand?
No need — all actual building happens on-site starting August 7.
What should we bring?
Laptops, chargers, and a valid college ID are mandatory. Sleeping bags or pillows if you plan to rest. We'll provide power strips, high-speed WiFi, and a dedicated sleeping area for all participants.
What technologies are allowed?
Any language, framework, or toolset of your choice. Cloud APIs, open-source libraries, no-code tools — all fair game. The only rule: work done before the event starts does not count. Everything must be built fresh.
Is accommodation provided for outstation participants?
Accommodation is available for every participant . Select this option during registration. The campus dormitory is a 2-minute walk from the hackathon venue.
How are projects judged?
Projects are evaluated on: Innovation & Originality (30%), Technical Execution (25%), Real-world Impact (25%), Presentation & Demo (20%). Each track has its own panel of 2–3 expert judges. Final evaluation is a 5-minute demo + 3-minute Q&A.
When are results announced?
Results are announced at the Closing Ceremony on August 8 at 5:00 PM. All finalists will present at 2:00 PM. Prize distribution happens immediately after the ceremony.
